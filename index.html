Reykjavik, Iceland's vibrant capital, effortlessly blends modernity with natural beauty. Nestled between the North Atlantic Ocean and stunning volcanic landscapes, Reykjavik is a city of contrasts. Explore its charming streets adorned with colorful houses, indulge in its thriving culinary scene, and soak in the rejuvenating waters of the nearby geothermal pools. Reykjavik's rich cultural heritage from museums to lively festivals, ensures a memorable experience in this Nordic gem.

Akureyri

scenic northern

What to do:

  • Botanical garden
  • Whale watching

Akureyri, affectionately known as the "Capital of the North" in Iceland, is a picturesque town nestled along the tranquil shores of Eyjafjörður, the country's longest fjord. Surrounded by snow-capped mountains, Akureyri is a gateway to outdoor adventures, from hiking and skiing to whale watching. This charming town boasts colorful houses, a vibrant cultural scene, and welcoming locals, making it a delightful destination for both nature enthusiasts and culture seekers.
